About Me

I'm a Computer Science student at UC Santa Barbara with a unique background in biopsychology and neuroscience research. My work focuses on developing methodologies that bridge the gap between human cognition and artificial intelligence.

My research journey began in cognitive science and neuroscience, where I studied the effects of sleep on cognitive performance and developed validated questionnaires for advanced research projects. This foundation in understanding human cognition naturally led me to computer science, where I now apply computational methods to analyze psychological phenomena and develop AI systems that can enhance human cognitive capabilities.

Research Focus

Neuro-Symbolic AI

Developing hybrid architectures that combine symbolic reasoning's interpretability with neural networks' learning capabilities to enhance cognitive performance.

  • Cognitive modeling frameworks
  • Adaptive integration systems
  • Real-time cognitive enhancement

Personality Type Analysis

Creating methodologies for studying individual personality types using observable and scientifically verifiable measurements through computational approaches.

  • Observable measurement systems
  • Statistical validation frameworks
  • Individual difference modeling

  • Fluid dynamics analysis
